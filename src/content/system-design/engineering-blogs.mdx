posts from companies that operate at the scale where you can't
fake your architecture diagram. Pick by the topic you're
working on.

## How to use this list

Don't try to read them all. The pattern that works:

<Steps>
<Step n={1} title="Search by topic before you design.">
About to design a sync engine? Read Figma + Linear + Dropbox
first. About to shard a database? Read GitHub + Pinterest +
Uber Schemaless. Two hours of reading saves two months of
rediscovering.
</Step>
<Step n={2} title="Look for the operational specifics.">
"We chose Cassandra" is useless. "We chose Cassandra and
operational pain at 100M rows looked like X" is gold. The
good posts give you the second.
</Step>
<Step n={3} title="Cross-reference against the postmortems.">
A blog post tells you what they built; a postmortem from
the same company sometimes tells you how it broke. Both
sides of the story matter.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout variant="info">
Engineering blogs rot faster than papers. If a URL stops
working, search the title and "engineering blog" — most
posts get mirrored, archived, or republished.
</Callout>
