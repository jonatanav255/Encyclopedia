# Data mesh

Zhamak Dehghani's 2019 proposal: treat **data as a product**,
owned by the domain teams that produce it, with shared
self-serve infrastructure and federated governance.

A response to the bottlenecks of centralized data teams:
"the data team can't keep up with 100 product teams' needs;
let each team own and serve its own data."

## The four principles

<Steps>
<Step n={1} title="Domain-oriented ownership.">
Each domain team (orders, payments, search) owns the data
it produces, end-to-end. The "orders team" provides an
"orders data product" the rest of the company consumes.
</Step>
<Step n={2} title="Data as a product.">
Datasets are products with consumers, SLAs, documentation,
versioning. Discoverable, addressable, secure, governed.
</Step>
<Step n={3} title="Self-serve data infrastructure.">
A platform team builds the tooling (ingestion, storage,
catalog, governance) that domain teams use to publish their
data products without needing data-platform expertise.
</Step>
<Step n={4} title="Federated computational governance.">
Standards and policies are set globally (schema standards,
privacy, lineage requirements) but implemented by each
domain.
</Step>
</Steps>

## The problem it tries to solve

In many companies, the data team is a bottleneck:

- All data goes into a central warehouse.
- All transformations are written by the data team.
- New requests queue forever.
- Quality issues are everyone's complaint and no one's
  responsibility.

Data mesh: push ownership to domain teams. The orders team
knows their data best; they should also own publishing it as
a clean product.

## What a "data product" looks like

A data product has:

- **A defined contract**: schema, semantics, freshness SLA.
- **Documentation**: what each field means, how it's derived.
- **Quality guarantees**: tested for completeness, freshness,
  accuracy.
- **Lineage**: where each field comes from.
- **Owner and consumers**: who maintains it, who depends on
  it.

Tools that help: dbt (modeling), DataHub / Amundsen / OpenMetadata
(catalog), Great Expectations / dbt tests (quality),
OpenLineage (lineage).

## The self-serve platform

Domain teams don't want to build infrastructure. The platform
team builds:

- **Ingestion** patterns (CDC, batch, streaming).
- **Storage** standards (Iceberg, Delta).
- **Compute** abstractions (Trino, Spark on demand).
- **Catalog** for discovery.
- **Governance** policies enforced by the platform.

A new domain team's "publish a dataset" should be a few
hours of work, not weeks.

## Federated governance

Centralizing governance (a single committee approving every
schema) is back to the bottleneck. Decentralizing it
completely (each team does whatever) creates chaos.

Federated: shared **standards** (every product must have an
owner, a freshness SLA, a privacy classification). Each
domain enforces locally.

A "governance committee" sets the standards; doesn't review
every change.

## What data mesh isn't

### "Just dbt + ownership"

That's part of it, but data mesh is broader: the platform,
the federation model, the explicit product framing.

### A specific tech stack

Mesh is architectural and organizational. Tech stacks vary
(Snowflake-only meshes, lakehouse meshes, polyglot meshes).

### A replacement for a central warehouse

Many meshes have a "centralized" warehouse holding everyone's
data — but ownership is per-product, not per-team.

## When mesh fits

- **Large orgs** with many domain teams, each producing
  meaningful data.
- **Strong platform engineering culture** willing to build
  shared infra.
- **Data is a competitive asset** with real investment.

When it doesn't:

- **Small companies** with one or two data teams. Centralized
  is fine.
- **Without strong platform investment**: domain teams won't
  build data products on their own.
- **Without governance commitment**: the mesh becomes 100
  inconsistent datasets.

## Common antipatterns

### "Mesh by org chart"

Splitting central data into 10 teams, calling each a "data
product owner." Without the platform, governance, or product
discipline, you've just decentralized the bottleneck.

### Mesh without quality

Each team publishes a "data product" with no tests, no SLA,
no documentation. Consumers don't trust any of them.

### Centralized mesh

A "platform team" that actually does all the work, with
domains nominally owning. The "mesh" label is marketing.

## Modern adoption

In practice, "data mesh" in 2026 looks like:

- dbt + Snowflake/lakehouse for transformations.
- A catalog (DataHub, Atlan, Collibra) for discovery.
- Domain teams own their dbt models.
- A small platform team maintains the warehouse and tooling.
- Cross-team standards enforced via CI checks.

It's "decentralized ownership with platform support," not a
specific architecture.

## In summary

- Data mesh: domains own their data products; platform team
  provides self-serve infra; federated governance.
- Response to centralized-data-team bottlenecks.
- Four principles: domain ownership, data as product,
  self-serve infra, federated governance.
- Requires platform investment + governance discipline +
  product mindset.
- Not a replacement for warehouses; a way to structure
  ownership.
- Adoption in 2026: dbt + catalog + lakehouse + clear
  ownership.
